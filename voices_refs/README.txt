REFERENCE VOICE CLIPS
=====================

Drop a clip in here and Chatterbox will clone it. Whatever you put here becomes
your channel's voice, so it has to be audio you actually hold rights to.

Three sources that are genuinely clean:

  1. YOUR OWN VOICE
     30 seconds on a phone is plenty. No licensing question at all, and nobody
     else on YouTube has it. Read anything - a paragraph of your own script is
     ideal because it matches the register you actually need.

  2. MOZILLA COMMON VOICE   https://commonvoice.mozilla.org/
     Released CC0 - an explicit public-domain dedication, so cloning is fine.
     Thousands of speakers in English, German and Spanish. Look for a warm,
     unhurried older male reader.

  3. LIBRIVOX               https://librivox.org/
     Public-domain audiobook recordings. Check the individual recording, since
     a few readers add their own terms. Plenty of measured, mature narrators,
     which is the right register for a 60+ audience.

WHAT MAKES A GOOD REFERENCE

  - 30 seconds or more of continuous speech (under 8s clones poorly)
  - One speaker, no music, no background noise
  - Calm, level delivery at the pace you want your videos read
  - Same language as the videos it will narrate

NAMING

  Name clips by language so they are easy to pick:
      english-narrator-1.mp3
      german-narrator-1.mp3
      spanish-narrator-1.mp3

Run this to test one:
      python3 make_video.py benchtts --lang en
